Boba / Bubble Tea

A lot of my friends love bubble tea, which have chewy tapioca pearls ("bubbles"). It's popular around many college campuses, but due to the COVID-19 pandemic, many things like classes are online and/or businesses have closed in the meantime.

If you don't have access and/or the funds for boba, it's possible to make it yourself!! It's super easy and quick too.

You can buy tapioca pearls and instant milk tea online. A friend and I have tested a few instant milk teas; we found that Casa Milky Flavor Teas and 3:15PM to be the best brands for bubble tea. There are other good ones, but cost wise don't work out.

  • For the Casa Milky Flavor Teas, we suggest the Okinawa (brown sugar) and Hokkaidou Sapporo varieties.
  • For 3:15PM, we suggest the Original and Earl Gray ones.
    The Rose one is okay, but risky choice if you never tried any rose milk tea before.

Ingredients list

  • 1/2 cup tapioca pearls
  • 16 oz of desired tea (minimum amt)
  • ~ 1 tsp - 1/4 cup of desired sweetener*
  • 5 cups water (per 1/2 cup of pearls)
* suggested sweeteners: sugar (white/granulated or brown); simple syrup; honey (regular/not raw); maple syrup

Instructions

  1. Fill a small or medium pot with 5 cups, or enough to fill the pot 2/3rds up (this can be eyeballed!)
  2. Let water come to boil at high heat
  3. Pour in the tapioca pearls
  4. Slowly stir until all the pearls float to the surface (~1-2 min), making sure that pearls don't stick together
  5. Lower the heat to medium and cover for ~ 5 minutes
    • At this point, there's some variation depending on your desired texture!
    • This part is a work in progress as I need to make sure my times are as accurate as possible.
  6. Drain the pearls using a strainer and rinse with cold water. The pearls should still be warm
    • For springier boba: fill a bowl with cold tap water and let the pearls rest for ~30 seconds
    • For firmer boba: use cold water from the fridge instead of tap
  7. Put the pearls in a bowl and cover it with the desired sweetener (note: multiple sweeteners can be used, ie maple syrup and a little bit of brown sugar works well)
  8. Gently stir and let sit for ~2-3 minutes
  9. Put desired amount of pearls into your cup of tea and enjoy!
